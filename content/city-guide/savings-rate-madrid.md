Madrid is one of Spain's highest-cost cities, but how does it stack up for savers? Using household expenditure data, this page breaks down typical monthly costs in Madrid and what savings rates look like for mid-income professionals living there.

## Madrid Cost Tier at a Glance

Madrid sits in the high cost tier relative to other Spanish cities. Total typical monthly costs come to €2,300, split between housing and all other living expenses. That figure serves as the baseline for calculating realistic savings rates at various income levels.

## Median Rent in Madrid

Median rent in Madrid reached €1,200 per month in 2023, making it among Spain's highest rental markets. However, it remains well below comparable Western European capital cities. Rent accounts for roughly 52% of the total typical monthly cost of €2,300, making housing the single largest line item for most residents.

## Other Monthly Expenses

Beyond rent, typical other expenses in Madrid amount to €1,100 per month. This category covers food, transport, utilities, healthcare, and discretionary spending. Combined with median rent, the total typical monthly cost lands at €2,300.

## Savings Rate Benchmarks for Madrid

Madrid's mid-income earners face a moderate housing burden, and savings benchmarks are considered achievable at typical professional salaries. To estimate your personal savings rate, subtract your total monthly costs from your net monthly income, then divide by your net income. With total typical costs at €2,300, a professional earning €3,000 net per month would have a theoretical savings rate of around 23% — though individual circumstances vary. Data not available for median net income by profession in Madrid.

## How Madrid Compares

Within Spain, Madrid's cost profile is high relative to cities like Valencia or Seville, where rents and living costs are lower. Compared to Western European peers such as Paris, Amsterdam, or London, Madrid's €1,200 median rent is notably more affordable. This positions Madrid as a mid-range city on a European scale, with savings potential that is competitive for the region. Data not available for direct city-to-city savings rate comparisons.

## Key Takeaways for Savers in Madrid

Total typical monthly cost in Madrid is €2,300, with rent at €1,200 and other expenses at €1,100. Housing represents the dominant cost pressure. Savers who can reduce rent — through flat-sharing or choosing outer districts — have the most direct lever for improving their savings rate.
