## What a Savings Rate Actually Measures

A savings rate is the percentage of your net (after-tax) income that you set aside rather than spend. It is calculated as: (Income minus Expenditure) divided by Income, expressed as a percentage. A higher savings rate means a greater share of income is being retained, which accelerates wealth accumulation and financial resilience. Most personal finance frameworks treat a savings rate of 20% or above as a strong target, though what is achievable varies significantly by location, income level, and household composition.

## How to Benchmark Your Own Savings Rate

Regardless of where city or national averages sit, your personal savings rate is the most actionable figure. To calculate it, take your total monthly or annual after-tax income, subtract all spending (fixed costs like rent, variable costs like groceries, and discretionary spending), and divide the remainder by your income. Multiply by 100 to get a percentage. Tracking this figure over time gives you a clear signal of whether your financial position is improving, holding steady, or deteriorating relative to your own baseline.
