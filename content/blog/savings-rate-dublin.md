Dublin is one of the most expensive cities in Europe, and its cost structure puts real pressure on household savings rates. With median rent above €2,000 per month and total typical monthly costs reaching €3,650, building a meaningful savings buffer requires deliberate planning. This page breaks down the numbers so you can benchmark your own position.

## Dublin's Cost of Living at a Glance

Dublin sits in the very-high cost tier. Median monthly rent stands at €2,100, making it the second-highest monthly rent in the EU as of 2023. Beyond rent, typical other monthly expenses — covering food, transport, utilities, and discretionary spending — come to €1,550. Combined, a typical Dublin resident faces total monthly costs of €3,650 before any savings are set aside. These figures reflect a city where fixed outgoings consume a large share of take-home pay across most income levels.

## How Rent Shapes Your Savings Rate

Rent is the single largest lever on savings rates in Dublin. At €2,100 per month, it accounts for roughly 57% of the total typical monthly cost figure of €3,650. For mid-income earners, this compression between income and rent means that achieving a standard benchmark savings rate — often cited as 15–20% of gross income — is structurally difficult without an above-average salary. Dublin renters face the country's most compressed income-to-rent ratio, and mid-income earners typically fall below common savings benchmarks as a result.

## Strategies That Matter Most in a High-Cost City

In a very-high cost tier city like Dublin, the highest-impact variable is housing cost. Reducing rent through house-sharing, relocating within commuter distance, or transitioning to ownership at a lower monthly payment than equivalent rent can shift a savings rate significantly. Beyond housing, the €1,550 in other monthly expenses offers limited but real room for optimisation — particularly in discretionary categories.
